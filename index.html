The Prison Creative Arts Project

"The Prison Creative Arts Project gives direct access to the writing of PVCC students who are completing an associate degree in General Studies while at the same time being incarcerated in Virginia’s prisons..."

"Our perspective on the lives, experiences, and backgrounds of the incarcerated traditionally has been informed and shaped, more or less exclusively, by information derived from popular culture—from TV and movies. However, the essays, poems, stories, and other writing made available here..."

"...give us unfiltered,

first-hand access to the truth

about these people’s - these students’ - unique lives,

people who otherwise have been hidden from view,

out of sight and out of mind."

-Ben Sloan, Project Coordinator & Writing Instructor

This writing is made available to the public thanks to a generous grant from Virginia Humanities.
